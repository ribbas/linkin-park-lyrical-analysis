The source code for the extraction and analysis of the data can be found in [this repository](https://github.com/sabbirahm3d/linkin-park-lyrical-analysis). The source code for generating the dataframes and plots in this notebook are exported out to increase readability. The plots for the analyses were generated with [plotly](https://plot.ly).

Multi-Platinum and Diamond certified, multi-Grammy and multi-Billboard Awards winning band Linkin Park is one of the best-selling music artist in the world. The band is known for experimenting with their musical styles, from bringing old school nu metal to the limelight to pioneering in futuristic electronic rock.

Photo Creds: Linkin Park Association

This Notebook analyzes the lyrical content of their songs. The lyrics ranged from politically charged to nuclear warfare topics. Most of their songs, however, consisted of topics on internal struggles and emotional abuse. Those subjects were more apparent in their earlier work, but still managed to be embedded in most of their songs.

The following analyses are generated on the lyrics: bag-of-words/term-frequency analysis, cosine similarity analysis and sentiment analysis.

Data¶

The tracklist and lyrics were obtained using a separate library closed off from this project. The package utilized Spotify API, BeautifulSoup4 and Tor. Its source is closed off because of the lyrics hosting website's instructions on its robots.txt explicitly forbidding any spiders.

Because 80 songs do not contribute significantly to traffic, the data were scraped cautiously and responsibly. The Tor network was used to mask the spider with a different IP address after every hit, and the pages were crawled with sufficient delays. Here's the snippet that generates the delay time:

time.sleep(random.uniform(5, 20))  # sleep for a random float between 5-20 seconds

Basic endpoints of the Spotify API were used to generate the tracklist before crawling.

Only songs from studio albums were considered. Songs from remix or collaboration albums, or featured songs were not included in the dataset to filter external influences from their lyrics. Songs with less than 10 lines of lyrics were removed. The final dataset consisted of 77 songs from 7 studio albums.

An array of hyphen-delimited Linkin Park album titles is used to generate the analyses on the individual albums

The text is then normalized through several layers of cleaning.

Using a sample string, the filtering process can be demonstrated. Let's pick a tough one:
"Ain't nothing more that'll have you grinnin like a possum eatin a sweet tater than these 80 songs."
(Translation: "There's nothing more that can make you happier than these 80 songs.")

The text goes through all the cleaning methods in the sequence described. The methods include:

Lowercase conversion
Original: "Ain't nothing more that'll have you grinnin like a possum eatin a sweet tater than these 80 songs."
Cleaned: "ain't nothing more that'll have you grinnin like a possum eatin a sweet tater than these 80 songs."

Expansion of word contractions
Original: "ain't nothing more that'll have you grinnin like a possum eatin a sweet tater than these 80 songs."
Cleaned: "is not nothing more that will have you grinnin like a possum eatin a sweet tater than these 80 songs."

Stripping punctuations (except parentheses)
Original: "is not nothing more that will have you grinnin like a possum eatin a sweet tater than these 80 songs."
Cleaned: "is not nothing more that will have you grinnin like a possum eatin a sweet tater than these 80 songs"

Stripping numerical values
Original: "is not nothing more that will have you grinnin like a possum eatin a sweet tater than these 80 songs"
Cleaned: "is not nothing more that will have you grinnin like a possum eatin a sweet tater than these songs"

Penn Treebank Parts of Speech Tagging [[1]](#ref1)
Original: "is not nothing more that will have you grinnin like a possum eatin a sweet tater than these songs"
Cleaned: [('is', 'VBZ'), ('not', 'RB'), ('nothing', 'NN'), ('more', 'JJR'), ('that', 'WDT'), ('will', 'MD'), ('have', 'VB'), ('you', 'PRP'), ('grinnin', 'VBP'), ('like', 'IN'), ('a', 'DT'), ('possum', 'NN'), ('eatin', 'VBZ'), ('a', 'DT'), ('sweet', 'JJ'), ('tater', 'NN'), ('than', 'IN'), ('these', 'DT'), ('songs', 'NNS')]

WordNet Lemmatization [[2]](#ref2)
Original: [('is', 'VBZ'), ('not', 'RB'), ('nothing', 'NN'), ('more', 'JJR'), ('that', 'WDT'), ('will', 'MD'), ('have', 'VB'), ('you', 'PRP'), ('grinnin', 'VBP'), ('like', 'IN'), ('a', 'DT'), ('possum', 'NN'), ('eatin', 'VBZ'), ('a', 'DT'), ('sweet', 'JJ'), ('tater', 'NN'), ('than', 'IN'), ('these', 'DT'), ('songs', 'NNS')]
Cleaned: ['be', 'not', 'nothing', 'more', 'that', 'will', 'have', 'you', 'grinnin', 'like', 'a', 'possum', 'eatin', 'a', 'sweet', 'tater', 'than', 'these', 'song']

Special case filtering methods

  • Stripping off dates
  • Grabbing strings in between paranthesis
  • Ignoring punctuation stripping for special words, i.e. C++

Analysis¶

The following variables map to arrays of dataframes or individual dataframes of their corresponding textual analysis:

  • rel_freq: Relative frequency analysis
  • cos_sim: Cosine similarity analysis
  • cos_sim_all: Cosine similarity analysis on songs across all albums
  • doc_sent: Aggregated document sentiment analysis
  • phrase_sent: Phrase sentiment analysis
  • extreme_phrase_sent: Outliers of phrase_sent
  • valence_arousal: Valence-arousal regression

Relative Frequency Analysis¶

Vocabulary of n-gram range [2, 8] were generated using a count vectorizer, with the following configuration:

    tokenizer=data.textfilter.normalize_text,
    max_features=2000,
    ngram_range=(2, 8),
    stop_words="english",
)

Emotion Classification ¶

The valence-arousal model of emotion suggests that emotions are distributed in a two-dimensional circular space, containing arousal and valence dimensions.[[5]](#ref5) The 4 quadrants of this 2-dimensional model is visualized below:

In [18]:

The Valence and Arousal Facebook Posts by Preotiuc-Pietro et al. provides a dataset of Facebook posts annotated on two independent scales, valence (or sentiment) and arousal (or intensity).[[6]](#ref6) The dataset is used to train a random forest regressor, which is used to predict the valence and arousal of the test data with an 80% prediction interval. The regressor was configured as follows:

sklearn.ensemble.RandomForestRegressor(

Limitations to Consider¶

  • Emotional classification itself is highly subjective. The valence vs arousal dimension model of affect is one of the several accepted dimensional models of emotion. The field of textual emotion detection is still very new and the literature is fragmented in many different journals of different fields.
  • The Modelling Valence and Arousal in Facebook posts dataset is composed of Facebook posts, which consists of the irregular and unstructured formats found in social media data. Normalizing those posts excluded several samples or stripped large portions of some posts.
